Emotional Intelligence

10/07/2021

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is a methodology that covers the areas of knowledge that one has regarding the recognition, control, evaluation and expression of emotions in both themselves, and other people that they interact with. This can be used to improve the identification of emotions, evaluating how other people are feeling, the management and balancing of one's own emotions, determining the best response to how other people are feeling, fabricating the right balance of emotions in one's social communication, and finding the best way to relate to other people.

How is it different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient, unlike emotional intelligence, prioritises and focuses on areas that relate to ones knowledge over areas such as visual and spatial processing, information about the world, the fluidity of their reasoning, memory capacity in both short term and working categories, and quantitative reasoning. When we typically think of intelligence, we think of how smart someone is, rather than how good of a communicator they are. This is what usually seperates the 2 concepts, as a good communicator doesn't necessarily have to be the most knowledable in what they're discussing, and one who is well verse in their field of expertise isn't always a strong communicator.

Why is it important?

The importance of emotional intelligence are the net benefits that it provides in different categories of emotional dialogue and processing. On a personal level, it can help bring stability to one's management of their own emotions. On an interpersonal level, it greatly improves ones communication skills through the identification of other people's emotions and allows one to improve their relationships with other people through empatheticly evaluated responses. On a business/academic scale, emotional intelligence is key to establishing a safe and healthy environment amongst colleagues and clients. Emotional intelligence is also a desirable trait to have for one who finds themselves in a leadership role, as they can much more easily manage both their emotions, as well as their workforces. Overall, EQ is a valuble resource, as we are all human at the end of the day, and humans are social beings. Therefore, mastering an asset such as EQ that analyses, investigates, and responds to human emotional dialouge, will provide you with a skillset that has the potential to exceed that of purely IQ alone.
