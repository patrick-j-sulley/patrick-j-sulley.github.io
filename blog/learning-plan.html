New Bootcamp Learning Plan

Long term goal/Career pathway

My long term goal throughout my journey down this career pathway, is to eventually become a full time remote full stack web/software developer. We have learnt that throughout COVID, industries that are primarily based in the digital landscape have the capability to operate at near or even full capacity on a work from home basis. The benefits of the remote approach to a profession have always interested me, such as the freedom of living in a location of choice over one of a closer distance to my workplace, the removal of the time investment of travelling to and from an office every day resulting in more free time to pursue other ambitions and interests, and the luxury of working in my own comfortable space. During bootcamp, I want to develop a full palette of both front and back end skills, as I would like to leave EDA where potential creative output has been increased to a level where I can be relied upon to merge into any development team and contribute in a big way. I also see myself reaching a point where I take my experiences and knowledge after a long and ambitious career, and pass that down to the next generation in the form of teaching, as I believe in giving back and take great pleasure at seeing those that I have taken under my wing grow and flourish. Destination wise, I would like to start off my career within the Wellington region, as I think the developer community based here is very collaborative and tightly knit. Long term, I would like to potentially explore the world and work internationally.

Strengths/Limitations in bootcamp

I am very skilled in getting into a routine and isolating my focus on a particular subject and seeing that through until the very end. If I am given a set of tasks to achieve, I become immersed in those tasks and fully dedicate myself to make it through all the obstacles that come along the way. I love working within teams and establishing a strong collaborative workflow through the building of good rapport that ensures everyone is comfortable and in it to see everybody involved succeed. My limitations involve natural learning barriers that have come alongside my neurological condition, such as verbal & non verbal communication issues, misinterpretation of information, and delays in my development. These are areas that for the most part I have the ability to work through, but as they come naturally to me, there will be times where they can take over my learning process without me noticing. Other limitations include my hesitancy to ask for help when needed in times of which Iâ€™m struggling, as well as my forgetfulness to give myself a break or to take time away from my learning when I need to recover. Throughout foundations, these strengths and limitations have mostly remained the same. Entering bootcamp, I see myself still being able to forge myself a routine that enables me to isolate my focus in any tasks I have been given, and throughout working with my cohart during foundations, I've fortunately been able to retain my ability to build good rapport with my colleagues and establish a very friendly and collaborative work environment. Limitations wise, I have been actively working on reducing my overall hesitancy to asking others for help, as I have had no shame in getting assistance from my fellow cohart members whenever I have been stuck. My natural learning and communication barriers still remain, but I have the mindset required to work through them adequetely.

Non technical/Human skills I would like to develop at EDA

I want to finetune my communication style so that I am as clear and concise as possible, as I have the tendency to go into too much detail and to repeat myself unnecessarily. I want to ensure that my work schedule is as balanced within a day to day basis as possible (i.e. Reduce the amount of work that is left until the last minute). I would also like to learn how to sell myself and what I have to offer in the best way possible to an employer. Heading into bootcamp, I will continue to work on combatting my limitations, such as clarifying information that I am not 100% certain of the meaning of, by asking for help when oppurtunity presents itself. I would still very much like to finetune my communication to avoid my natural tendency to ramble and repeat myself. Improving my self awareness and reflective skills would also be very advantagous, as I can often get lost in my work and forget to reflect and give myself recovery time when necessary.

Expectations of the bootcamp team

Based on my conversation with members of the team at this stage, I have the expectation that they will respond to any difficulties I am facing with empathy and compassion, and that they will work alongside me to help me work through the obstacles I am facing. I also have the expectation that they provide me feedback & constructive criticism in areas where I need to improve, so that I can immediately identify where I need to step up and take action. Entering bootcamp, these are still the expectations I have.

Expectations on myself in Bootcamp

I will managed my workload in a proactive and productive manner, in which I will stay engaged, work hard, seek help when I need it, give help to my fellow students when they need it, give myself the appropriate balance of work/leisure to reduce burnout, maintain a high level of attendance, be very communicative with my peers and the bootcamp team, and most importantly, enjoy myself. I also will carry the expectation that I jump up for any community oppurtunites to begin networking myself and meeting people within the industry.
