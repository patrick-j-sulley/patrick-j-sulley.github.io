Foundations Reflection

25/07/2021

3 new things I've learnt about myself through core learning?

Firstly, the concept of neuroplasticity and how I can use that to work around my natural learning limitations. Secondly, whenever I am in a situation where I need to reflect back on what somebody has said to me (Listening & Looping), focusing on memorizing key terms is a good strategy that I can employ whilst actively listening. Thirdly, ego wise, I would say that the core learning confirmed to me that I am an open minded person who operates on growth, being self-critical, and empathetic to others.

Roles of values, empathy, and self awareness in learning & programming.

Having a stable set of strong values allows you to be more certain of what outcomes you are striving. I.e. if you have critical thinking as a core value, then you are more likely to approach your learning and work with a troubleshooting strategy in order to rule out outcomes that you find to not be the most efficient or logically sufficient.

Empathy in learning and programming relates to one's ability to work and collaborate with others. A person with a strong empathetic mindset is more likely to work well in a team based environment, as they are able to help contribute more to a friendly setting that enables people to work comfortably.

Self-awareness allows you to identify your working style, your communication style, and enables you to be constructively self-critical in order to identify areas that you have the capacity to build upon and improve.

What has surprised me the most about core learning?

I would say that learning about neuroplasticity and how being able to use it as a boosting element to a growth mindset. I have always understood that the developer landscape is an ever-growing one, so having the correct mindset is crucial if you want to keep up is important. But, learning about the inner workings of a scientific concept that can help you knowingly work around your neurological limitations was very helpful for me to uncover.

Most challenging aspects of core learning?

Some of the concepts touched upon, such as the aforementioned neuroplasticity, can be difficult ones to practice and get comfortable with. Especially from the perspective of someone with a neurological disorder that directly affects both my verbal and nonverbal communication and learning skills. However, the payoff to the challenge at hand is valuable, as learning techniques that directly build upon my natural barriers enables me to be a better communicator and thinker.

Why do I think EDA focuses on core learning as much as they do?

Above all else, the target outcome of this bootcamp is to promote graduates into the workforce, and the web development industry, like most team and office environment based industries, is one that focuses on people skills over technical skills. Your ability to work within a team of like-minded people is what ultimately sells you to an employer.

Does my time spent studying the core learning feel like a waste?

Not at all. Again, coming from somebody who is very open about their neurological disorder and past with mental health issues, areas that build upon non-technical skills are almost always helpful. Even for somebody not in my shoes, building upon your people skills and your learning skills are ways that you can always make up for shortcomings that you may feel like you have in your technical abilities.
