Neuroplasticity

How does neuroplasticity benefit people?

Through the focused mental engagement that you are able to tap in to thorough understanding the principles of neuroplasticity, you are able to improve both the speed and efficiency in your learning as you will configure your neural networks to much more easily work with whatever related task at hand you are attending to.

Engaging with neuroplasticity for my own benefit.

In the context of the foundations and the bootcamp, I plan to engage with these principles through both physically and mentally immersing myself in my code in a number of different ways. (i.e. Using breakpoints and analyzing the focused line of code to understand the purposed functionality, physically writing any example code given or found in order to build upon muscle memory etc..)

Ways to increase neuroplasticity

Practicing through Repetition. Relating newly observed information and contextualizing it in different ways. Consistent reminders that the capacity of knowledge is not limited, and not knowing something doesn't equal not being able to know something. Confusion implies an incomplete connection. Growth mindset

What is it? Why is it Relevant?

Growth mindset is the idea that knowledge is ever expanding through a lifetime. New habits, new information, new abilities, new techniques, new skills can all be learnt and obtained. A fixed mindset implies that the current capacity of knowledge that one has is incapable of being grown. As a developer, having the perspective that you can always learn more, is a very strong mindset to have, as the development world is ever expanding, and the ability to stay engaged and to keep up is an important asset.

Changes or Surprises?

This exploration didn't surprise me, as employing this mindset throughout my learning over the years has helped me in a great manner. The evidence of this, for me, is seeing how I learnt prior to this mindset, compared to my learning after.

Blending in with my learning plan & strategies.

Over the years, through the application of employing a growth mindset, I have learnt and applied methods to address my natural learning and communication barriers that are symptoms of my Asperger's. I will continue to apply this mindset when it comes to my learning and communicating at EDA.

With my long term career goal of being a remote based developer in mind, I intend to grow as comfortable as possible working from home as possible in order to achieve maximum efficiency within that environment.

In order to meet my goal of finetuning my communication style, I intend to practice talking to my fellow students and facilitators, and to seek feedback on my communication abilities when possible. Practicing through repetition.

In relation to coding, both neuroplasticity and a growth mindset will be incredible relevant. Building up muscle memory for the process of typing coding is important, as is gaining outside perspective through asking for help or feedback from team members when you ask them to look at your code. A growth based mindset removes the intimidation of approaching newer technologies and languages within web development, and enables you to increase your portfolio/experiences, leading to more potential job opportunities later down the road.
